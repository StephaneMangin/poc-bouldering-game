# Vision Propositions

## Option 1: Accessible Simulation
**Tagline:** `Realistic climbing, without excessive frustration.`

1. Player promise: feel the real logic of climbing (route reading, placements, fatigue), with smart assists.
2. Target audience: sport/technique-curious players, casual to mid-core.
3. Tone: immersive, understated, personal progression.
4. Differentiator: strong compromise between realism and immediate fun.
5. Risk: may feel "in-between" if balancing is off.

## Option 2: Arcade Tension
**Tagline:** `Short, intense, replayable runs.`

1. Player promise: climb fast, take risks, beat your records.
2. Target audience: score/challenge/speedrun players.
3. Tone: fast-paced, stylized, performance-oriented.
4. Differentiator: flow sensation, short sessions, central leaderboard.
5. Risk: less "simulation" depth for purists.

## Option 3: Vertical Adventure
**Tagline:** `Every cliff tells a story.`

1. Player promise: explore unique walls with weather, atmosphere, and light narrative goals.
2. Target audience: exploration + immersion players.
3. Tone: contemplative + punctual tension.
4. Differentiator: strong artistic and emotional identity.
5. Risk: heavier production (environments, narration, staging).

## Recommendation
**Option 1 (Accessible Simulation)** is recommended for a first game:
it provides a solid identity, remains achievable as an MVP, and can evolve towards arcade or adventure later.

## Proposed Vision (starting point)
1. Vision: deliver the most readable and satisfying climbing experience possible, where technique matters more than raw speed.
2. Player fantasy: "I read the route, I choose my holds, I control my effort, I reach the top."
3. Key experience: progressive physical tension + satisfaction of a clean move.
4. Target: 5 to 15 minute sessions, visible progression every run.
