# Player Fantasy

## Promise
The player must feel they are progressing by reading the wall, choosing their footholds, and managing their effort — not by spamming actions.

## Core Statement
"I read the route, I choose my holds, I control my effort, I reach the top."

## Target Experience
1. Progressive physical tension: every move has a cost and a consequence.
2. Technical satisfaction: a good movement sequence is more effective than an aggressive approach.
3. Readability: the player understands why they succeed or fail.
4. Personal progression: run after run, they climb more cleanly.

## Target Audience
1. Casual to mid-core players who enjoy technical but accessible games.
2. Players who appreciate short sessions (5 to 15 minutes).
3. Players motivated by mastery, not only by speed.

## What the Game Must Avoid
1. Arbitrary punishment without clear feedback.
2. Controls too complex in the first minute.
3. Purely arcade or purely simulation gameplay without a compromise.

## Validation Indicators
1. The player understands the objective and basic controls in under 2 minutes.
2. The player identifies at least one placement mistake after a failure.
3. The player wants to restart a run to do better, not just to finish.
